What can you find in a square meter if you observe it for a year?

A very natural question to wonder about, of course. So, at the beginning of this year (2026), I set out to find the answer.

Hello!

I am Sri Harsha, the human behind this project. I spend about 2â€“3 hours every day on this square plot, right in my backyard in Gundmi village.

I pick up my camera, crouch near the plot, and stare hard at the square meter plot. After a while, usually, space and time split open and a bug appears! Then I end up kneeling, lying flat on the ground, or somehow standing in a way that hurts my knees a lot, just to take pictures of this bug.

Then I spend another hour or so staring hard at my computer screen, skimming through these photos, usually shaking my head in disappointment.

Then it takes about an hour more to figure out what exactly this bug is. As usually happens, I end up 11 links deep into Wikipedia or iNaturalist listings, then wonder why it is night already. Such is the nature of this job. And then I do it all again the next day.

Well, I am a happy Sisyphus.

Even though the temptation is high to move a leaf around or gently scoot the bug toward better-lit areas to get a good shot, I have chosen the noble path of restraint so far. Here are the rules I follow.

I like bugs. I like the struggle of taking a good picture. And I like constraints.

This project is very similar to that of by Chris Helzer, which I learnt about much later after starting this. He is an inspiration.

If you'd like to say hi or send bug reports (heh!), especially about misidentifications, please send me an email.
